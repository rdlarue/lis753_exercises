As of Oct. 5, more than 2 million mail ballots had been requested. Election authorities in Illinois are allowed to process vote-by-mail ballots as they receive them, but they can’t calculate any totals until polls close at 7 p.m. on Nov. 3. On election night, voters will likely see the counts for all mail ballots that were received and processed before Election Day. Mail-in ballots have until Nov 17 to be counted.
The State Board of Elections consistently posts the number of unreturned mail ballots reported by election authorities statewide. This allows voters to see the potential for vote totals to change for both national and local elections.
